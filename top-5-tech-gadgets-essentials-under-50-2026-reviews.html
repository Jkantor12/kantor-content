A simple, effective lighting upgrade that makes a visible difference from the very first use.

If you have ever joined a video call and noticed how much better some people look on camera, proper lighting is almost certainly the reason. This ring light delivers a clean, even glow that flatters faces and eliminates the harsh shadows that overhead room lighting tends to create. Setup is refreshingly quick — clip it on, plug it in via USB, and you are ready. The three color temperature modes (warm, cool, and daylight) are genuinely useful rather than just a marketing feature, and the brightness dial gives you fine control. The clip mechanism is sturdy enough for most monitors and laptop screens. It is not a professional studio light, and it will not replace a proper lighting rig for serious video production, but for everyday calls and casual content creation, it absolutely gets the job done at a price that is hard to argue with.

  • Three color temperature settings with smooth dimming
  • USB-powered — no separate adapter needed
  • Compact and easy to store or travel with
  • Noticeably improves on-camera appearance right away
  • Light ring diameter is small, limiting spread for wider setups
  • Clip may not fit thicker monitor bezels securely
  • USB cable length could be longer for some desk arrangements

Who it's for: Remote workers, students, and casual content creators who want to look polished on video calls without investing in a full lighting setup.

5. USB-C Hub — Multi-Port Adapter for Laptops and Tablets

The connectivity solution that modern slim laptops desperately need — practical, well-built, and easy to use.

The trend toward thinner laptops with fewer ports has created a very real everyday problem for people who need to connect multiple devices at once. This USB-C hub addresses that gap by expanding a single USB-C port into several useful connections, typically including USB-A ports, HDMI output, SD card slots, and pass-through charging. The build quality stands out at this price — the aluminum shell feels premium and dissipates heat better than plastic alternatives. It works reliably with MacBooks, Windows ultrabooks, and compatible tablets, though it is worth checking your specific device for any power delivery limitations. The HDMI output supports up to 4K, which is genuinely useful for connecting to an external monitor. No installation is required — plug it in and everything works, which is exactly what a hub should do.

  • Aluminum build runs cooler and feels noticeably more durable
  • HDMI output supports 4K resolution for external displays
  • Truly plug-and-play with no software installation needed
  • Expands one USB-C port into several genuinely useful connections
  • Power delivery wattage may not satisfy all high-performance laptops
  • USB-C cable attached to hub is fixed and not replaceable
  • Does not include an Ethernet port, which some users will miss

Who it's for: Laptop users — especially those with newer slim models — who need to connect external monitors, USB drives, cameras, or other peripherals without juggling multiple adapters.
