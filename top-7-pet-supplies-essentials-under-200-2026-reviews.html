The double-layer honeycomb design traps litter between the layers rather than just sitting on top, which means it actually stays put until you shake it out over a trash bin. The material is soft enough that cats do not avoid it the way they sometimes avoid stiffer mats. It lays flat without curling, which is a surprisingly common problem with cheaper alternatives. That said, it does require regular shaking to stay effective, and larger cats with bigger paws tend to scatter litter past the mat's edges. Still, for the price and convenience, it is hard to argue against it.

6. Slow Feeder Bowl

An elegantly simple solution to a very real health problem — fast eating — that works exactly as described.

If your dog inhales meals in seconds flat, a slow feeder bowl is not a luxury — it is genuinely important for their health. Eating too quickly can lead to digestive discomfort and, in larger breeds, more serious complications. This bowl uses an internal maze pattern to force dogs to work around obstacles to reach their food, naturally slowing the eating process. The difference in mealtime duration is noticeable from the very first use. The bowl feels solid and heavy enough that most dogs cannot simply flip it over out of frustration. It is also dishwasher safe, which makes cleanup effortless. The one limitation is that it works better with dry kibble than wet food, as wet food can fill the grooves and become difficult to clean thoroughly.

  • Immediately and noticeably slows eating pace
  • Sturdy design resists tipping and sliding
  • Dishwasher safe for easy cleaning
