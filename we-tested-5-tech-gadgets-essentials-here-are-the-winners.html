We Tested 5 Tech & Gadgets Essentials — Here Are the Winners

TopPick Staff Review • Updated 2026

The tech and gadgets space has never moved faster than it does right now. In 2026, the sheer number of options on the market can feel genuinely overwhelming — whether you are building out a home office setup, upgrading your daily carry, or just trying to cut through the clutter and find tools that actually work as advertised. We spend a lot of time separating the genuinely useful gadgets from the ones that look impressive in a product listing but disappoint in real-world use. After hands-on testing across several weeks, we narrowed our focus to five essentials that cover the broadest range of everyday needs.

When evaluating tech essentials, we focus on a few non-negotiable qualities: build quality that holds up over time, performance that matches the marketing claims, and value that makes sense for a real household budget. We also pay close attention to ease of setup, because a gadget that requires an engineering degree to configure is not actually a useful gadget for most families. With those standards in mind, here is what we found after putting each of these five products through its paces.

1. Smart Plug

A surprisingly capable smart home entry point that earns its place in any connected home setup.

Smart plugs have been around for years, but the quality gap between budget options and genuinely reliable ones remains significant. After testing this model for several weeks, we found it delivers consistent performance without the frustrating connectivity dropouts that plague cheaper alternatives. Setup through the companion app took less than three minutes, and voice assistant integration worked smoothly on the first attempt. The scheduling features are intuitive enough that anyone in the family can configure them without help. Our one ongoing concern is that the physical footprint is slightly bulkier than competing models, which can block an adjacent outlet depending on your wall plate configuration. That said, for the price point, the reliability and feature set are genuinely hard to beat.

Who it's for: Anyone taking their first steps into smart home automation or looking for a dependable replacement for an unreliable existing plug.

Pros

  • Quick, straightforward app setup

  • Compatible with major voice assistants
  • Solid energy monitoring feature built in

Cons

  • Bulky design can block adjacent outlets

  • No Bluetooth backup if Wi-Fi goes down

2. USB-C Hub

A well-rounded hub that solves the port shortage on modern laptops without breaking the bank.

If you own a modern laptop, you already know the frustration of having two or three USB-C ports and a constant need for more connections. This hub addresses that problem with a practical selection of ports that covers most real-world scenarios. Data transfer speeds tested at consistent and reliable rates across multiple file types, and the 4K HDMI output handled an external monitor without any noticeable lag or color issues. The build feels sturdy rather than hollow, which is a meaningful distinction at this price. The one area where it falls short is heat management — during extended use with multiple high-draw devices connected simultaneously, the hub gets noticeably warm. It has never caused a problem in our testing, but it is worth noting for users planning intensive setups.

Who it's for: Remote workers, students, and creatives who need reliable multi-device connectivity from a single laptop port.

4. Portable Battery

A dependable power bank that handles family travel days and long outings with genuine confidence.

Portable batteries are one of those products where reliability matters far more than flashy features, and this one has reliability in abundance. Over the course of our testing, it charged a smartphone from nearly empty to full multiple times before needing a recharge itself, which aligns well with its listed capacity. The dual-port design means two people can charge simultaneously, a practical feature for families and travel companions. Fast charging support worked as expected with compatible devices. The weight is on the heavier side compared to slimmer alternatives, so it is not ideal for ultralight packing situations, and the included cable is shorter than we would prefer. For general travel, events, or days away from outlets, though, this portable battery is exceptionally dependable.

5. Streaming Microphone

An accessible entry into quality audio recording that delivers results well above its price point.

For content creators, remote presenters, and anyone who has grown tired of muffled laptop microphone audio, this streaming microphone is a genuinely exciting step up. Plug-and-play USB setup means there are no drivers to install and no configuration headaches — plug it in and your computer recognizes it immediately. The recorded audio has a warmth and clarity that transforms video calls and recordings without requiring any post-processing work. The cardioid pickup pattern does a commendable job of isolating your voice from background room noise. The microphone arm mount included is functional but on the flimsy side, and the gain control knob could benefit from more precise calibration at lower levels. Neither issue undermines the core audio quality, which remains the main reason to recommend this product.

Who it's for: Podcasters, streamers, remote workers, and students who want noticeably better audio quality without investing in a professional studio setup.

Pros

  • True plug-and-play with zero driver installation

  • Attractive, professional appearance on camera

Cons
